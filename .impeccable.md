# Design Context — Central Gate Estates

## Project
East London lettings and property management agency. Registered in Fitzrovia, portfolio concentrated in Shoreditch, Bethnal Green, Hackney, Hoxton, Whitechapel, Bow. Built for people who live and work in E1, E2, E8, EC1, EC2, N1.

## Target Audience
- **Tenants**: 25–38 year olds. Creative, tech, or trade workers. Know their area, don't take bullshit. Rent £1,400–£3,000/month. Primarily mobile. Respond to directness.
- **Landlords**: 35–65. Busy professionals or small portfolio investors. Want reliability, transparency, no surprises. Not Mayfair clients — East London landlords with real properties.
- **Applicants**: People actively flat-hunting. They're tired of Rightmove. They want a local agent who actually knows the street.

## Brand Personality
Urban. Honest. Community-rooted. Think: the sharpest local agent on the block who knows every planning application in E2, who drinks in the same pubs as their tenants, who doesn't charge you for breathing. **Not** Mayfair luxury. **Not** discount/budget. **Not** corporate. Confident without being brash.

Reference points: Dezeen (editorial), Brutalist London (aesthetic), The Modern House (photography), Factual (no fluff).

## Design Direction: Urban Editorial
**Unforgettable element**: Property listings that feel like editorial spreads. The site should feel like picking up a beautifully designed local magazine about East London — not using a database search tool.

**Tone visual equivalents**: Exposed brick. Warehouse glass. Canal reflections. Terracotta. Concrete. Warmth within rawness.

## Typography
- **Display / Headlines**: Syne (geometric, contemporary, used by creative studios — not on the banned list). Bold (700/800). Uppercase for section labels, title-case for headlines.
- **Body / UI**: DM Sans (clean, warm, readable — not Inter/Roboto/Arial). 400/500/600 weights.
- **Accents / Eyebrows / Labels**: Syne 600 uppercase, tracked wide (0.1em+), small (11–13px). Used for section labels, badge text, nav items.
- **Property prices**: Syne Bold — the number is the star.
- **Type scale**: Use fluid clamp() for marketing pages. Fixed rem for dashboard/app.
- **Line length**: Cap at 65ch for body. Headings can be wider.

## Colour Philosophy
- `#1a1a1a` Charcoal — backgrounds, primary text, headers. Confident, grounded.
- `#f5f2ee` Cream — warm white. Not clinical. Used for light sections, card backgrounds.
- `#c4622d` Terracotta — the brand accent. Used sparingly but boldly. Brick, warmth, East London heritage. CTAs, active states, accent lines, hover underlines.
- `#4a6fa5` Slate Blue — trust signals. Used for secondary actions, info badges, compliance markers. Less prominent than terracotta.
- `#2a2a2a` / `#111111` — deep backgrounds for hero sections, dark cards.
- `#8a7968` Warm Taupe — muted text, captions, timestamps. Warm, not cold grey.
- Avoid: pure white (#fff), cold greys, navy, gold, any gradient that looks like a SaaS dashboard.

## Spacing Philosophy
- Generous whitespace. Sections breathe. Don't cram.
- Use asymmetry deliberately: text starting at col 2 of 12 while images bleed to edge.
- Consistent internal rhythm: 8px base unit. Common gaps: 16, 24, 32, 48, 64, 96, 128px.
- Section padding desktop: 96–128px vertical. Mobile: 64px.
- Card padding: 24–32px. Compact but not cramped.

## Component Aesthetic
- **Buttons**: Primary = terracotta fill, cream text. No border-radius > 4px (not bubbly). Uppercase label, tracked. Secondary = transparent, charcoal border, 1.5px. Ghost = no border, underline on hover.
- **Cards**: Cream bg, 1px taupe border. No box-shadow by default (flat). Subtle drop shadow on hover. Image aspect ratio locked 4:3.
- **Inputs**: 1px charcoal border, cream bg. Focus: 2px terracotta ring. No rounded corners > 4px.
- **Tags/Badges**: Uppercase, 11px, Syne, tracked. Status: available=green-tinted bg, let=slate bg, let-agreed=amber bg.
- **Horizontal rules**: 1px taupe. Used as decorative section dividers. Sometimes terracotta for emphasis.
- **Hover states**: Subtle. Border colour shift, or a thin terracotta underline animation for links. No scale transforms (feels toyish).
- **Animations**: Restrained. Fade-up on scroll for section entries (150ms ease-out). No parallax. No floating elements.
- **Icons**: Lucide — 20px, 1.5px stroke. Never filled. Never coloured unless specifically meaningful.

## Layout Patterns
- **Hero**: Full-viewport height. Photography edge-to-edge. Headline left-aligned, not centred. Dark overlay (60% charcoal, subtle texture grain). Text max-width 60%.
- **Property cards**: Horizontal list on desktop (image left, details right) OR vertical grid. NOT the standard Rightmove-clone 3-col grid of equal cards stacked predictably.
- **Section headers**: Eyebrow label (terracotta, all-caps Syne) above headline. No centred section headers unless absolutely necessary.
- **Stats/numbers**: Large. Bold. Syne. Let the number dominate.
- **Testimonials**: Single quote per view, large. Pull-quote style. Author with small photo.

## Anti-Patterns (never do)
- Generic blue primary buttons
- Centred hero text in a rounded card
- 3-equal-column card grids with identical padding
- Times New Roman defaults
- "Lorem ipsum" or obviously AI-generated copy
- Generic stock smiling-people-in-office photography
- Gradient overlays that look like SaaS dashboards
- Bounce animations or over-the-top transitions
- Shadcn/ui default theming without customisation
- Purple, teal, or lime green accent colours
- Cards with border-radius > 8px
- Section titles like "Our Services" or "Why Choose Us" without a subhead that earns it

## Imagery Notes
Use Unsplash images of: Shoreditch street scenes, Brick Lane, canal-side (Regent's Canal), warehouse conversions, Victorian terraces, east London markets. Warm-toned. High contrast. Real people/places, not stock office photography.
